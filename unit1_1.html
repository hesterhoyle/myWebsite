1.1 - Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

In the modern world, more and more businesses have come to depend on IT for a variety of reasons. A large part of businesses swapping over to IT is that it is much faster, effect and more cost effective for them to use. The main focus of a business is to make profit, therefore by utilising IT systems, they can widen their profit margins further. For example, imagine having a list of customers. Without IT systems, the list would most likely be filed on paper. To locate one customer out of that database, you would have to physically search through to find them, which takes time. With a digital database, you can just search through in a matter of seconds to find the customer information.

Not only do IT systems allow a business to be more efficient within itself, it also allows it to become more global. Through the internet, a business is able to reach a much wider audience and potential customer base. Not only can you contact people from around the world, you can also collaborate and share resources and information too.

1.2 - Describing methods, skill and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully

With such a wide range of resources available, there are many ways to complete digital projects successfully. First of all, it is important to identify the best software and hardware needed to make your project a success. So for example, in the context of this website, to create it I have used HTML and CSS. The benefit of these are that they allows control over each part of the site, and allow me to customise it to my standards and tastes. HTML and CSS are also great for if you want to expand on the site further, as it allows greater flexibility. Different digital projects require different things, so it is important to first of all identify what you want to achieve.

Further resources that will help you with your digital projects are communication tools to allow a team to work together efficiently. These might be apps or sites like Slack, which allows teams to communicate and share ideas, or GitHub, which allows teams to work on a single project online together.

1.4 - Describe the risks that might impact digital projects

Some of the biggest risks that you can face with digital projects is, of course, hardware issues. This might include the internet going down (meaning you cannot access any content you have online, or contact members of your team), or it could be that your computer breaks. These risks can be minimised by having regular and multiple back-ups both online and offline.

Another risk of digital projects is that new technology is always being developed. This means that what you are developing may suddenly need to be updated/changed to work on a newer system (e.g. new Apple iOS). Although you cannot always plan for these kinds of issues, it is important to be prepared or at least be prepared to adapt to the situation.

1.5 - Describe how you would go about selecting and using IT systems and software

For any business, selecting the right kinds of IT systems and software is important. There’s no point in buying the newest and fanciest tech if it is not relevant to your business.

First of all, you should define the problem and work out what exactly needs to be done. You should think about the workflow and how it will be used day-to-day. Are you looking for something that will allow staff to collaborate together online? Are you looking for something that can track inventory? Decide which features you cannot live without.

The next stage is to research various solutions. Think about whether you need just one solution or if you may need multiple. Are there any other features not related to day-to-day use that you absolutely need? At this point it is also important to compare a few different options to decide which might work best. Getting feedback from your team can also help. If you’re all mostly agreed on what is needed then you can be sure that that feature is important.

Finally, weigh up the cost of the system. Do you really need a new software or system? Is it efficient for the business to purchase it? Just because something is the newest does not necessarily mean is the best for your business’ situation.

1.7 - Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects

The Companies Act 2006 requires businesses to disclose certain pieces of information on their websites. It should be easy for people to find. These pieces of information are things such as the company's name, their registered phone number, registered office address, email addresses, VAT numbers etc.

In May 2018, GDPR regulations came into effect. This legislation covers how personal information of customers or users is processed and accessed.

Websites should be accessible to everyone who needs it, according to the Equality Act 2010. This includes things such as being compatible with commonly used assistive technology (e.g. screen magnifiers, speech recognition tools) and including people with disabilities in user research.

All websites and their content are inherently copyright protected, as long as they are original works. Businesses should be aware of copyright and avoid using material that belongs to someone else’s copyrights (e.g. using someone else’s images without permission)
